Ticket Priority Levels & SLAs

Below is the definition for each priority level to be considered while raising the SE Support tickets.

P1 - Emergency

  • Any task completion or service required to remove any deal blockers associated with opportunities closing on the same day.
  • Up to 30 minutes to be contacted.

P2 - Critical

  • The SE requires quicker attention and response.
  • The SE needs the deliverable for the next hours or within the same day.
  • Expected response in 1 hour from SE Support.

P3 - Normal

  • The SE have a standard request.
  • The SE needs the deliverable within the next 24 hours.
  • Expected response in 3 hour from SE Support.

P4 - Low

  • The SE have a request considered as low priority.
  • The SE needs the deliverable within the next 24-48 hours.
  • Expected response in 4 hour from SE Support.

Escalation

  • The SE has not received a first contact response or updates during the first 12 hours.

Ticket Information & Processing

Mandatory Information on the ticket

  • A. System Engineer Name - Enter the name of the SE who is creating the ticket.
  • B. Ticket Type
  • C. Priority - Set the correct priority (refer the priority definitions shared above).
  • D. Account Name - Currently a manual field, enhancements to auto-populate Account Name coming soon.
  • E. Opportunity - not mandatory (if available).
  • F. Subject
  • G. Status - will default to New.
  • H. Description - Provide complete information to help the SE Support Team resolve the ticket quickly.
  • I. Attachment - Attach all the relevant documents under "Related" tab once you save the ticket.

Processing Submitted Tickets

Once a ticket has been submitted, the System Engineers can use the Feed tab on the case object in SFDC to communicate and provide further details and updates on the case. The SE Support Team will utilize the Send email feature on the case object to work with various stake holders for a communication and resolution.
